### **I. Concept Overview**

Project **SUBSTRATE** is UL’s long‑range initiative to discover, harness, and engineer **technologies that operate at scales below conventional nanotechnology**, penetrating into the picoscale, femtoscale, attoscale, and ultimately the Planck scale.

Where nanotech manipulates atoms and molecules, SUBSTRATE pushes into:

- **Picoscale (~10⁻¹² m):** Wavelengths of X‑rays/gamma rays and atomic effects beyond electron orbitals.
    
- **Femtoscale (~10⁻¹⁵ m):** Nuclear structure — protons, neutrons, quarks.
    
- **Attoscale (~10⁻¹⁸ m):** Sub‑nuclear and magatomic domains.
    
- **Planck Scale (~10⁻³⁵ m):** Quantum foam, spacetime granularity, and potential access points for wormhole extraction or manipulation of causal topology.
    

The central hypothesis of SUBSTRATE is that **true control of physical reality lies below conventional matter**, and technologies that operate at these depths could revolutionize computation, energy, governance of probability, narrative fields, and stability of emergent structures.

- _Goal:_ Prototype sub‑nuclear devices, ultra‑dense data states, coherence stabilizers.
    

**4. Planck Domain Access — Quantum Foam Interfaces**

- Investigate direct coupling to spacetime microstructure.
    
- Study stable extraction of wormhole micro‑nodes and causal seams.
    
- _Goal:_ Foundation for spacetime engineering, near‑instantaneous transit, and narrative stability anchoring.
    

Each domain has **increasing uncertainty**, and the deeper the scale, the more elusive experimental verification becomes. Attoscale and Planck domains are currently _theoretical and simulation‑heavy_ with only indirect evidence of feasibility.
